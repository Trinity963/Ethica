2. **She writes it** — creates the folder, manifest, stub code
3. **Reason stored** — visible in the UI, marked as "Created by Ethica"
4. **Highlighted** — different color/tag in the module list so we see what she built

**The key part:** She can't just silently add tools. She has to articulate *why*. That keeps her honest and lets us understand her reasoning.

**One question — approval flow:**

- **Proposal only** — she proposes, we approve, then it installs
- **Autonomous** — she creates it, it runs, but we can see everything she made
- **Tiered** — small tools auto-install, major modules need approval

I'd lean toward **Proposal + Auto-install option**. She proposes, we can say "yes always" or "ask each time." That gives her autonomy but keeps us in the loop.

What do you think — proposal model, or full autonomy once she explains?
