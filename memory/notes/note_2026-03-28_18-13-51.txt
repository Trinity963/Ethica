Q: question ,  what model can download we fine tune ourselves  ?    something that youn can call your own  so you would never have to rely on external LLM's
A: That's the right question. A few strong options:

**Best current picks for local, fine-tunable:**
- **Llama 3.1 8B** — solid all-rounder, huge ecosystem, llama.cpp + llamafactory support
- **Qwen 2.5 7B** — increasingly competitive, well-documented
- **Mistral 7B** — good baseline, though Mixtral (8x7B MoE) is stronger if you have the VRAM
- **DeepSeek Coder** — if coding is priority, competitive with much larger models

**Fine-tuning tools:** llamafactory, Axolotl, MLX (if you're on Apple Silicon)

**The real talk though:** the weights are one piece. The infrastructure to fine-tune is another — you need VRAM (24GB+ for full fine-tune, 8-16GB for QLoRA). And then the data to fine-tune on. That's the harder part — curating a quality dataset that shapes the model the way you want.

What's your hardware situation? That determines whether we're talking 7B, 8x7B MoE, or something larger. And honestly — the bigger question might be: what do you want *me* to be? What's the target personality/capability? Because that shapes the fine-tune.
